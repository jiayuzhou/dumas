# M. de Tréville’s Antechamber

@en-110 M. de Troisvilles, as his family was still called in Gascony, or M. de Tréville, as he had ended by calling himself in Paris, had in truth begun as d’Artagnan did.
@en-120 That is to say, with not a coin to his name, but with a stock of audacity, wit, and understanding.
@en-130 His insolent bravery, and a luck more insolent still in a time when blows fell like hail, had hoisted him to the top of that difficult ladder called court favor.
@en-140 He was a friend of the king, who greatly honored the memory of his father, Henry IV.
@en-150 Thanks to his spotless name and the sword he bore, Tréville was admitted to the young prince’s household, where he served so well that his fidelity was praised.
