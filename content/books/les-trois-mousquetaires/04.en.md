# Athos’s Shoulder, Porthos’s Baldric, and Aramis’s Handkerchief

@en-010 Furious, d’Artagnan had crossed the antechamber in three bounds and was darting down the stairs, when, carried away by his speed, he ran headfirst into a musketeer coming out by a side door.
@en-020 The blow, forehead to shoulder, wrung from the latter a cry—indeed, a howl.
@en-030 “Excuse me,” said d’Artagnan, trying to resume his course, “excuse me, but I’m in a hurry.”
@en-040 He had scarcely descended the first flight when an iron grip seized him by his sash and stopped him.
@en-050 “You’re in a hurry!” cried the musketeer, pale as a shroud. “Under that pretext you jostle me, you say ‘Excuse me,’ and think that’s enough? Not quite, my young man.”
@en-060 “Upon my word,” replied d’Artagnan, “I didn’t do it on purpose; I said, ‘Excuse me.’ That seems enough; still I repeat it—on my honor!—I’m in haste, very much in haste.”
@en-070 “Sir,” said Athos, releasing him, “you are not polite. One sees you come from far.”
@en-080 “Morbleu, sir!” said d’Artagnan. “However far I come, it won’t be you who gives me lessons in good manners.”
@en-090 “Perhaps,” said Athos.
@en-100 “Ah! if I weren’t so pressed,” cried d’Artagnan, “and if I weren’t running after someone…”
@en-110 “Monsieur the man in a hurry, you’ll find me without running—near the Carmes-Deschaux, toward noon.”
@en-120 “Toward noon? Good; I’ll be there,” d’Artagnan replied.
