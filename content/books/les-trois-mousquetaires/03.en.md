# The Audience

@en-010 At the moment, M. de Tréville was in a very bad mood; nevertheless he politely greeted the young man, who bowed to the ground, and he smiled on hearing his compliment, whose Béarnais accent reminded him at once of his youth and his country — a double memory that makes a man smile at any age.
@en-020 But almost at once, moving back toward the antechamber and motioning to d’Artagnan, as if to ask leave to finish with the others before beginning with him, he called three times, each time raising his voice, somewhere between the imperious and the irritated tone:
@en-030 “Athos! Porthos! Aramis!”
@en-040 The two musketeers we already know at once left the groups to which they belonged and advanced toward the office, whose door closed behind them as soon as they had crossed the threshold.
@en-050 Their bearing, though not quite calm, nevertheless by its easy dignity and submission stirred d’Artagnan’s admiration; he saw in these men demigods, and in their chief an Olympian Jupiter armed with all his thunderbolts.
@en-060 When the door had closed behind them and the buzzing murmur of the antechamber had begun again, M. de Tréville paced in silence, brows knit, the full length of his office; then, stopping suddenly before them, he covered them with an angry look.
@en-070 “Do you know what the king told me,” he cried, “no later than last night? Do you know it, gentlemen?”
